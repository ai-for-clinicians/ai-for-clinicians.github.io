Clinical questions

How clinicians currently search relevant clinical evidence to help their patients. Why systematic reviews and guidelines are really important to them, but frequently outdated. How creating these documents is a meticulous, time-consuming process and why they're outdated when they come out already. How the evidence ecosystem is not meeting the needs of stakeholders: Precise, relevant information that helps my particular patient.

Improving evidence search

How many people have built lots of tools to make evidence search easier and faster. What good ideas they already had. How tools still fall short of what clinicians and reviewers need. How developing tools is often hard, as previous work cannot be re-used. How we do not know how design decisions affect search outcomes.

A common infrastructure

How many people have been dreaming of making the world more interoperable. Some history of the semantic web. The re-birth of the field in the shape of the knowledge graph. How some people push fairly grand visions for the future, while others try to just get something started.

A vision for the future

How the clinical knowledge graph could get started. Populating and maintaining the graph. Governance and ensuring future viability. Not having to anticipate all possible use cases, but instead allowing extensibility. Getting buy-in from essential stakeholders. Allowing a gradual phase-in, rather than a wholesale change.
